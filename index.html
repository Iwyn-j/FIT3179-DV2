Global Meteorological and Air Quality Visualization

Global Weather Map

The visualization depicts the temperature distribution in countries for 2023, measured in degrees Celsius. Regions with darker blues, such as Canada and Russia, experience cooler temperatures. In contrast, areas near the equator display lighter shades, indicating warmer climates. Australia is notably cooler, while the Antarctic region is absent from the temperature representation. This map provides a clear overview of global temperature variations during the year.

Average Temperature

The graph presents a comparison of average temperatures, in Celsius, for various countries. The countries range from Bahrain to the United Arab Emirates. Notably, all displayed countries exhibit average temperatures above 30°C, indicating warm or tropical climates.

PM2.5 Concentration: A Look at the Top 10 Countries

The graph showcases the average PM2.5 levels across ten countries. China appears to have the highest concentration, significantly surpassing the others. The values indicate varying air quality conditions, with higher PM2.5 values suggesting poorer air quality.

Comparative Humidity: An Overview of Various Countries

The chart presents the average humidity percentages across a diverse range of countries. From the data, we observe fluctuations in humidity levels, with some countries experiencing near-maximal humidity, while others, like the United States of America, exhibit significantly lower levels.

Weather Patterns: A Diverse Spectrum of Conditions

The pie chart offers a visual representation of various weather conditions. The vast array of segments highlights the diversity and frequency of each condition, with some, like the dominant pink segment, signifying a prevalent weather type, while many others indicate a multitude of less frequent conditions.

Cross-Country Analysis: Wind Speed and Average Temperature

The scatter plot showcases a comparison of average wind speed (in mph) against average temperatures (in °C) for a diverse range of countries. Each colored dot represents a specific country, while its size signifies the number of records or data points available. This visualization aids in discerning climatic patterns and variations across nations.
